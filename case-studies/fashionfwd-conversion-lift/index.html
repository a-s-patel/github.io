FashionFwd Conversion Lift: Product Page Overhaul

A CRO sprint that reworked product context, trust, and sizing flow.

Published Feb 2026 · 6 min read · by Proof Over Hype

Context (what was happening)

FashionFwd saw strong paid traffic volume but conversion stalled after a seasonal assortment refresh. Product pages were heavy on imagery yet light on proof, causing hesitation and abandoned sessions on mobile.

Constraints (budget, timeline, what couldn’t change)

The team had three weeks, no engineering bandwidth for full rebuilds, and no changes allowed to pricing or shipping policies. We could only adjust layout, content blocks, and on-page merchandising.

Hypothesis (what you believed + why)

We believed conversion would lift by reducing decision friction: surface size guidance earlier, add trust signals near the add-to-cart area, and simplify the product narrative into a scannable, benefits-first layout.

What We Changed (bulleted list)

  • Moved size guide and fit notes above the fold on mobile.
  • Added a four-point trust bar (shipping, returns, materials, care).
  • Collapsed long descriptions into a three-tab structure.
  • Inserted a UGC carousel directly below the primary image.
  • Replaced generic copy with benefit-led bullet highlights.

The Experiment Plan (5–7 steps)

  1. Audit scroll depth, click heatmaps, and drop-off by section.
  2. Draft a modular product page layout with three content blocks.
  3. Launch an A/B test across top 12 SKUs for two weeks.
  4. Monitor conversion, add-to-cart rate, and returns rate daily.
  5. Iterate on UGC placement and sizing language at day 7.
  6. Roll out the winning layout to remaining core products.

Results (table with before/after; use reasonable placeholder metrics)

Metric Before After
Conversion Rate 2.1% 3.0%
Add-to-Cart Rate 7.4% 9.6%
Mobile CVR 1.6% 2.4%
AOV $98 $101
Return Rate 8.2% 8.0%

What Worked / What Didn’t

The biggest lift came from the early sizing guidance and UGC placement, which reduced back-and-forth scrolling before add-to-cart. The trust bar also improved mobile engagement in the first 20 seconds.

The tabbed layout needed clearer labels. The first variant buried fabric care details, which increased support tickets; we adjusted the labels to make care info more visible.

Repeatable Takeaways (bullets)

  • Front-load sizing and fit to prevent hesitation loops.
  • Place UGC where customers decide, not just where they browse.
  • Structure long copy into scannable blocks with outcomes first.
  • Monitor support tickets alongside conversion for hidden friction.
