Landing Page Teardown

A teardown checklist for clarity, trust, and conversion improvements.

Published Feb 2026 · 7 min read · by Proof Over Hype

Landing pages fail when the promise is unclear, the proof is thin, or the path to conversion is friction-heavy. This teardown playbook is built for quick audits that isolate the highest-leverage fixes before you redesign.

Step 1: Clarify the core promise

Your headline should answer three questions in one scan: who this is for, what outcome it delivers, and how fast it works. If you need a paragraph to explain it, the promise is not clear enough.

Checklist

  • Headline names the audience or use case directly.
  • Subheadline explains the tangible outcome or metric.
  • The hero visual reinforces the promised result.

Step 2: Build trust above the fold

Visitors look for fast credibility signals before they read. Put the most convincing proof close to the CTA so they do not have to scroll to believe.

Checklist

  • Add one specific stat, testimonial, or recognizable logo set.
  • Use short, concrete proof (numbers, timeframes, or outcomes).
  • Keep claims consistent with the offer and pricing tier.

Step 3: Remove conversion friction

Every additional field or decision point adds drop-off. The best landing pages reduce choice and make the next step obvious.

Checklist

  • Primary CTA uses a single verb and a clear outcome.
  • Form fields map only to the next step (not full qualification).
  • Secondary CTAs are visually subordinate to the primary action.

Teardown workflow

  1. Capture a full-page screenshot and annotate every element.
  2. Score each block on clarity, trust, and friction (1 to 5).
  3. Rewrite the hero and CTA before changing layout.
  4. Test one change at a time and measure the lift.

Measurement guidance

Track conversion rate, scroll depth, and click-through on the primary CTA. Keep traffic quality constant during tests to avoid false positives.
